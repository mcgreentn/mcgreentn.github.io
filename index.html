We often think of AI as something that plays chess, identifies faces, auto-completes search results, or drives cars. AI is all around us — constantly studying patterns and using that knowledge to benefit us in some way.

We ask: What is possible for this AI to learn?
But rarely do we ask: What can we learn from this AI?

I hold a Ph.D. in Artificial Intelligence from New York University. My research uses video games as a domain to study how AI systems can be applied to teaching. I love video games, coffee, code, dogs, and sailing.
