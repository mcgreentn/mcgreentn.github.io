About Me

hi, I'm Michael Green

We often think of AI as something that plays chess, identifies faces, auto-completes search results, or drives cars, among a multitude of other things. AI is all around us, constantly being used to study patterns and then use that knowledge to benefit us in some way.

We ask: What is possible for this AI TO LEARN?
But rarely do we ask: What can WE LEARN from this AI?

I hold a Ph.D. in Artificial Intelligence from New York University. My research uses video games as one domain with which to study A.I, and how A.I. systems can be applied to teaching things. I love video games, coffee, code, dogs, and sailing.

Read my motivations for my research here.

View my thesis on Automated Video Game Tutorial Generation.

Data Agent

an open-data, murder mystery generator

Data Agent is a game within the genre of Open Data Games. Given a person on Wikipedia, the system automatically generates an entire murder mystery adventure to play using evolutionary algorithms. The game client can then load up this game for you to play!

Seer

time-travel interactive fiction

A text-based, narrative-adventure game about time-travel that I made for my Narrative Game Design Class in Spring 2018.

Olav Broccoli: Ghost of Space

a 3d, space-shooter prototype

After I created Cubes, I decided I wanted to build another space-themed prototype within a "next-gen" Stay Alive context. Olav Broccoli is not finished in the slightest, but it is fun to mess around with 3d space, and even more fun to optimize it for phones. The name comes from one of my good friends in college. It makes sense to us, which is what counts.

Downloadr

Before Spotify, I used to download all my music from youtube (mostly artists that released stuff for free like TheFatRat). Along the way I became exhausted with the heavy application based software and the online, ad-infested apps. So I made my own simple downloading tool.

The AtDelfi System

Automatically Designing Legible, Full Instructions

The beginnings of my thesis research, AtDelfi is a system contained within the GVGAI Framework which reads in source code to output a tutorial. The first AtDelfi paper, sent to FDG 2018, won the Best Paper Award of the conference.

MicroRCT

a theme park AI framework

MicroRCT is an open source simulator inspired by the theme park sandbox game RollerCoaster Tycoon. The goal in MicroRCT is to place rides and shops in an amusement park to maximize profit earned from park guests.

The GDMC Challenge

generative design in minecraft

The GDMC Settlement Generation Challenge is to produce an algorithm that is adaptive towards the provided map, creates a settlement that satisfies a range of functional requirement - and also looks good and evokes an interesting narrative. The goal is to produce an generator that can rival what humans can produce.

Lifeguard

what's "getting fired"?

A cyclical, text-based, narrative-adventure game about my favorite experiences from being a lifeguard in highschool.

Stay Alive: Evolved

a galaga-esque, action packed survival experience

The Stay Alive series was one of my very first video games, originally made for mobiLehigh 2014. It is a visually-overwhelming, 2D, space-ship shooter game. It is currently not found online, but I'm working on putting it up on itch.io!

Cubes

an experiment in 3d physics and racing

Cubes was a prototype I built my junior year of college for mobiLehigh 2015. We were interviewed about it. and were awarded the Bethlehem's Choice Award. Cubes was my first exploration into 3d space and physics within the Unity Engine.

Trumpland

a political Twitter simulation

Not as much as enjoyment as "unjoyment", Trumpland is a political satire game about Twitter in the age of Trumpism. Become the Don and tweet as he tweets. The more you match what he actually wrote, the more followers and likes you gain.

The GVG-AI Framework

general video game artificial intelligence

A incredibly easy and efficient way to test AI against a large variety of simple 2d video games. I have helped develop the Rule-Generation Track together with some of my colleagues.

2118: Corruption

a cyberpunk rpg prototype

I made this game with a team for my final project in my Narrative Game Design class in Spring 2018. The idea was to create a cyberpunk detective game using design techniques learned in class. I was the sole programmer on the team and thus was in charge of all programming and game mechanic related content. It's not much, but this was made in only 4 weeks!
